# Golavo v0.16.0 — A longer memory, and the season ahead

Golavo 0.16.0 makes the five pinned 2026-27 domestic schedules playable, extends the
browsable match record beyond 100,000 rows, and makes dense match evidence easier to read.
Competition-wide context, like-for-like team comparisons, and records that belong to only
one side now have separate visual homes. Match actions stay inside their cards at every
supported width, and the match directory can be browsed with a status or competition
filter without inventing search text.

This is also the first release in which a club prediction can enter the immutable
seal-to-score ledger. It retains a stricter grading boundary than internationals: Golavo
scores a club seal only when two independent result sources agree. With one accepted
result source today, the honest state is pending.

## What is new

- **The 2026-27 domestic season.** Complete pinned schedules for the Premier League,
  La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, and Ligue 1 unlock the 10,000-run seeded season outlook,
  score picks, and pre-match sealing. Schedule rows provide fixture identity and local
  clock labels; they never become training data.
- **A 100,525-match record.** Premier League history now reaches 1992-93 and Bundesliga
  history reaches 1963-64. The added CC0 footballcsv rows are browsable and searchable,
  but remain isolated from the modern league training view and cannot be sealed.
- **Golavo Ratings.** Model Lab includes a cutoff-safe national-team Elo table computed
  only from Golavo's pinned international results, with 12-month movement and sample size.
  It is Golavo's own estimate, not the proprietary FIFA ranking.
- **Golden Boot and shootout ledgers.** Supported international competitions expose
  scorer and penalty-shootout records at the requested cutoff. Own goals are not credited,
  and unsupported competitions return a typed no-data state.
- **Opt-in pre-kickoff weather.** An eligible upcoming match can fetch Open-Meteo context
  from the user's own machine. The capture is shown only when it predates kickoff, carries
  visible attribution, and never enters a model.
- **A measured model rejection.** `contextual_dixon_coles` tested club-specific home
  advantage and rest signals, lost the admission test, and is not seated on any scored
  council. Its negative result and diagnostics remain published in Model Lab.

## Easier match reading

The deeper-cut notebook now starts with competition context, moves to a direct home-versus-
away comparison, and then gives each team a distinct record lane. One-sided facts no longer
sit beside an unexplained blank cell. On narrow windows, paired rows become labelled team
cards instead of squeezing a desktop table into the viewport.

The match-card footer now owns the Follow and Correct controls. Its border, padding,
wrapping, and 44-pixel touch targets keep both actions inside the rounded card in Warm,
Light, and Dark themes. Match Search also treats Played, Upcoming, and competition filters
as valid browsing requests on their own, with a filter-aware empty state and direct reset.

## Trust boundaries

- Forecasts still train only on complete rows available before the fixture cutoff.
- The two deep-history packs are records, not new forecast inputs.
- Future schedules never train a model or certify a live result feed.
- Club grading waits for two independent sources to agree.
- Weather is user-triggered, display-only context.
- Lineups, injuries, suspensions, observed xG, and live event feeds remain unavailable.
- The macOS artifact is ad-hoc signed and is not Apple-notarized.
